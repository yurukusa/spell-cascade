## The One Rule

> **Publish page + upload files AT THE SAME TIME** (not separately).

Placement in itch.io's "Recent" section is based on page publish date. If you create the page early and upload files later, you waste the visibility boost.

## The 3 Pillars of itch.io Success

1. **Accuracy** (right tags, right genre, right description)
   - Determines who sees your game
   - Determines whether they click
   - Determines if they like what they find

2. **Visuals** (cover image, screenshots, UI clarity)
   - Guides 80% of click decisions
   - Establishes polish and quality expectation
   - Shows core gameplay at a glance

3. **Engagement** (respond to comments, post updates, show community)
   - Builds player loyalty
   - Generates word-of-mouth
   - Signals active development
   - Feeds into ongoing visibility via devlogs

Do these three well, and visibility follows.

## FAQ

**Q: Is "In Development" vs "Released" important?**
A: No. It's just a filter. No visibility boost/penalty for changing it. Set to "Released" if v0.2 is feature-complete.

**Q: Does changing the game later hurt me?**
A: No. Post a devlog for major updates instead. Devlogs get indexed and show in follower feeds, giving you new visibility.

**Q: Should I post 10 devlogs in the first week?**
A: No. This looks like spam and can trigger filters. Aim for 1 devlog on launch day, then 1-2 per month ongoing.

**Q: What if my game is small/free/low-effort?**
A: Doesn't matter. itch.io is for all games. Polish and accuracy matter more than scope.

**Q: Can I change tags after launch?**
A: Yes, but get them right initially. Tag changes don't reset discoverability.

**Q: Is "Name Your Price" better than "Free"?**
A: Slightly lower raw discoverability, but better conversion and revenue. For indie games, PWYW is recommended.

**Q: How many views/downloads should I expect Week 1?**
A: Depends on tags and promotion. Benchmark: 50+ views and 10+ comments = healthy start.

**Q: What if I get negative comments?**
A: Respond professionally, fix bugs if they're real, move on. itch.io doesn't moderate, so you need to manage comments.
